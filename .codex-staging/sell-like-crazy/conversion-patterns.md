# Conversion Patterns

Use these as adaptable scaffolds. Replace placeholders with real context and keep every claim truthful.

## 1. Positioning pattern

Pattern:
`[offer category] for [specific audience] who need [specific outcome] without [painful alternative].`

Use when:
- the offer feels broad or hard to place
- the page needs faster message match

Example scaffold:
`A [service/product/system] for [audience] who need [result] without [common frustration].`

## 2. Outcome-first headline

Pattern:
`Get [desirable outcome] without [major objection].`

Use when:
- the buyer already knows they have the problem
- speed and clarity matter more than intrigue

## 3. Audience + result headline

Pattern:
`For [audience] who want [result] without [tradeoff].`

Use when:
- relevance is more important than broad appeal
- the offer is niche or role-specific

## 4. Mechanism-driven subheadline

Pattern:
`Do it with [method/mechanism], so you can [result] without [old way].`

Use when:
- the buyer needs to understand why this is different
- the market is crowded

## 5. Problem agitation opener

Pattern:
`If you are tired of [pain], this is built to help you [result].`

Use when:
- the audience feels the pain sharply
- the copy needs a stronger emotional handshake

## 6. Offer stack

Pattern:
- `You get [core deliverable].`
- `Plus [supporting element or bonus].`
- `Plus [implementation/support/proof asset].`
- `So you can [desired outcome] faster or with less risk.`

Use when:
- the offer feels under-explained
- buyers need to see concrete value, not just a label

## 7. Risk-reversal pattern

Pattern:
`If [reasonable condition], we will [refund/remedy/support action].`

Use when:
- buyers hesitate because of uncertainty
- trust is lower than desired

Guardrail:
- only use guarantees the business can actually honor

## 8. Natural urgency pattern

Pattern:
`[Reason the window matters], so [deadline/constraint] is the best time to act.`

Use when:
- there is a real deadline, capacity cap, seasonal window, or pricing change

Guardrail:
- never invent countdowns or fake scarcity

## 9. CTA pattern

Pattern:
`[Verb] my [result/resource/next step]`

Examples:
- `Get my quote`
- `Book my walkthrough`
- `See plans`
- `Download the checklist`

Use when:
- the CTA is too generic or low-intent

## 10. Form explanation pattern

Pattern:
`Answer [small number] quick questions so we can [specific benefit].`

Use when:
- the form feels like a hurdle
- users need a reason to share information

## 11. Proof block pattern

Pattern:
`[Specific audience/result] using [offer/mechanism].`

Examples of proof structure:
- `Reduced [pain metric] by [amount]`
- `Reached [outcome] in [timeframe]`
- `Improved [process] without [tradeoff]`

Use when:
- claims need evidence nearby
- testimonials are too vague

## 12. Objection handling pattern

Pattern:
`Worried about [objection]? Here is how [offer] handles it: [specific explanation].`

Use when:
- buyers stall at a predictable concern
- FAQ sections feel passive or generic

## 13. Pricing frame pattern

Pattern:
`[Price] for [scope], including [important inclusion].`

Use when:
- price is shown without context
- buyers need help comparing value

## 14. Lead magnet pattern

Pattern:
`Get the [number]-point [asset type] to help you [specific result].`

Examples:
- checklist
- scorecard
- calculator
- template
- teardown
- buyer's guide

Use when:
- lead magnets feel vague or low-value

## 15. Email subject pattern

Pattern:
- `[result] without [pain]`
- `[question tied to buyer problem]?`
- `[short curiosity hook with clear relevance]`

Use when:
- open rates or reply intent are weak

## 16. Email opener pattern

Pattern:
`You are likely dealing with [pain]. If fixing [pain] would help you [result], here is the simplest next step.`

Use when:
- outreach or nurture emails feel too self-centered

## 17. Section-heading pattern

Pattern:
- `How it works`
- `What you get`
- `Who this is for`
- `Results customers see`
- `Common questions before you decide`

Use when:
- page sections sound like filler
- scannability is weak

## 18. Before-and-after pattern

Pattern:
`From [current frustrating state] to [desired state] with [mechanism/offer].`

Use when:
- transformation needs to be made tangible
- the buyer needs to feel momentum

## 19. Follow-up reminder pattern

Pattern:
`Still interested in [result]? Here is what happens next if you move forward today: [steps].`

Use when:
- leads need low-pressure reactivation
- next steps are unclear

## 20. Page opening sequence

Use this default order when rebuilding a page:

1. headline with audience + outcome
2. subheadline with mechanism or reason to believe
3. proof or trust signal near the top
4. benefits translated into outcomes
5. offer details and inclusions
6. objection handling and FAQ
7. CTA with next-step clarity
