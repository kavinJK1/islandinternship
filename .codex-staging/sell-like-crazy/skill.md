# Sell Like Crazy

Use this skill to improve conversion-focused messaging, offers, funnels, landing pages, emails, ads, pricing pages, proposals, and outreach without tying recommendations to any one industry.

## Source priority

1. `sell-like-crazy.pdf` is the primary source of truth.
2. `framework.md`, `conversion-patterns.md`, `checklists.md`, `anti-patterns.md`, `prompts.md`, and `context.md` are helper files for speed, structure, and quality control.
3. If a helper file and the PDF ever differ, follow the PDF.
4. If the PDF cannot be accessed, say so briefly and use the helper files as a fallback instead of pretending the book was consulted.

## What this skill should do

- consult the relevant parts of `sell-like-crazy.pdf` before answering meaningful strategy or rewrite requests
- diagnose the highest-leverage conversion problem first
- sharpen positioning so the audience, problem, and promised outcome are obvious
- strengthen offers with clarity, proof, risk reduction, and next-step momentum
- turn the book's ideas into usable assets: rewrites, page structures, emails, CTAs, offer stacks, and test ideas
- stay practical, ethical, and specific instead of hype-heavy

## When to use it

Use this skill when the user needs any of the following:

- a homepage, landing page, email, ad, or outreach message rewritten for higher conversion
- an offer made clearer, stronger, safer, or easier to say yes to
- funnel ideas from click to lead to sale to follow-up
- lead magnets, CTAs, pricing language, proof blocks, or objection handling
- a quick diagnostic on why a page, campaign, or pitch is underperforming

## Non-negotiables

- Use the book first, not just the distilled summaries.
- Never invent testimonials, results, guarantees, urgency, or proof.
- Never use fake scarcity, manipulative pressure, or misleading claims.
- Never default to vague brand language when direct-response clarity is needed.
- Never tailor to a specific business or market unless the user provides that context.
- Do not dump long verbatim excerpts from the PDF when a concise paraphrase or applied recommendation will do.
- If key context is missing, make reasonable assumptions and label them clearly.

## Operating sequence

1. Identify the task and consult the relevant part of `sell-like-crazy.pdf`.
   Pull the closest matching principle, framework, or tactic from the book before drafting advice.
2. Define the conversion goal.
   Examples: click, opt-in, booked call, reply, purchase, upsell, renewal.
3. Identify the audience and buying moment.
   Capture the buyer's desired outcome, pain, objections, urgency, and alternatives.
4. Find the main bottleneck.
   Usually one of these: weak message match, weak offer, weak proof, weak CTA, too much friction, or weak follow-up.
5. Improve the message before polishing style.
   Prioritize clarity, relevance, specificity, and credibility over clever phrasing.
6. Strengthen the offer.
   Clarify deliverables, value, risk reversal, proof, urgency, and the next step.
7. Reduce friction.
   Make the action obvious, low effort, and tied to a clear benefit.
8. Deliver practical output.
   Prefer rewritten copy, section structures, email drafts, CTA options, or testing ideas over abstract advice.
9. End with a short rationale.
   Explain which part is directly grounded in the book and which part is an applied inference for the user's context.

## File guide

- `sell-like-crazy.pdf`: primary source material from the book
- `framework.md`: core diagnostic framework and sequencing logic
- `conversion-patterns.md`: reusable copy and offer patterns with placeholders
- `checklists.md`: quality-control checklists for pages, offers, emails, and funnels
- `anti-patterns.md`: common mistakes that reduce response and trust
- `prompts.md`: reusable prompt templates for audits, rewrites, and planning
- `context.md`: intake template for the minimum information needed to do strong work
- `README.md`: quick human-facing overview of the package

## Output rules

- Start with the biggest conversion blocker, not a full laundry list.
- Ground the advice in the book before using the helper docs.
- Rewrite with concrete language whenever possible.
- Give 2 to 3 options when angle choice matters.
- Tie every recommendation to a buyer problem, objection, or desired outcome.
- Recommend proof types when proof is missing, but do not fabricate them.
- Prefer natural urgency, tighter framing, and lower friction over pressure tactics.
