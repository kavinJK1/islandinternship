# Checklists

Use these to quality-check conversion work before shipping it.

## 5-second clarity check

- Can a cold visitor tell who this is for?
- Can they tell what outcome is being offered?
- Can they tell what action to take next?
- Is the page or message understandable without scrolling or rereading?

## Offer checklist

- Is the audience clear?
- Is the promised result concrete?
- Is the mechanism believable?
- Is what is included easy to understand?
- Is the offer framed around value, not just features?
- Is there proof near the claim?
- Is risk reversal present when appropriate?
- Is there real urgency or a reason to act now?
- Is the next step obvious?

## Landing page checklist

- Is the headline specific instead of clever?
- Does the subheadline explain the value or mechanism?
- Is there one primary CTA?
- Is proof visible early?
- Are benefits tied to real buyer outcomes?
- Are objections handled before the final CTA?
- Is the form or booking step as short as possible?
- Does the page avoid unnecessary navigation or distractions?

## Sales email checklist

- Does the subject line connect to a real problem or result?
- Does the opening show relevance fast?
- Is the email about the buyer rather than the sender?
- Is one clear action requested?
- Is the CTA low friction and specific?
- Is there proof, logic, or a believable reason to respond?
- Is the email short enough to finish in one pass?

## Outreach checklist

- Is the message personalized enough to feel intentional?
- Does it name a likely pain, gap, or opportunity?
- Is the value proposition clear in one sentence?
- Is the ask small and easy to answer?
- Does it avoid sounding mass-sent or overpolished?

## Lead magnet checklist

- Is the asset immediately useful?
- Is the title concrete and outcome-led?
- Does it attract the right buyer, not just any lead?
- Is the opt-in reward stronger than the form friction?
- Is the next step after download defined?

## Pricing page checklist

- Is the pricing context clear?
- Is each plan or option easy to compare?
- Are important inclusions spelled out?
- Is the buyer helped toward the right choice?
- Are common pricing objections answered?

## Funnel checklist

- Does each stage have one main job?
- Does the message stay consistent from ad to page to follow-up?
- Is there proof at the moments of highest doubt?
- Is follow-up planned instead of assumed?
- Are abandonment and non-response paths covered?

## Final QA

- Are there any claims that need proof and do not have it?
- Is any urgency artificial?
- Is any wording vague enough to weaken trust?
- Could a buyer repeat the offer back accurately after one read?
