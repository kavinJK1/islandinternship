# Context Intake

Use this template when you want stronger output with less back-and-forth.

## Minimum context

- Audience: who the buyer is
- Goal: what action you want them to take
- Offer: what is being sold or promoted
- Problem: what pain or frustration the offer solves
- Desired outcome: what success looks like for the buyer
- CTA: what the next step is right now

## High-value context

- price or pricing model
- objections or buying friction
- proof assets already available
- channel or traffic source
- funnel stage
- constraints on tone, compliance, or claims
- competitors or alternatives buyers compare against

## Fast intake template

```text
Audience:
Goal:
Offer:
Problem:
Desired outcome:
Mechanism or differentiator:
Price or commitment level:
CTA:
Top objections:
Available proof:
Traffic source or channel:
Anything that must not be said:
```

## If context is incomplete

Default behavior:

- infer the likely funnel stage from the asset
- improve clarity and offer strength before suggesting design changes
- recommend proof types if proof is missing
- use natural urgency only when a real reason exists
- avoid industry-specific assumptions unless they are strongly implied

## Useful proof inputs

If the user has any of these, ask for them or use them:

- customer quotes
- before-and-after metrics
- screenshots or product walkthroughs
- case studies
- volume, usage, or performance data
- founder or team credibility
- guarantees or support policies

## Good input beats clever output

When the audience, offer, and desired result are specific, the copy becomes far more persuasive. If only one thing can be clarified, clarify the buyer and the next step.
