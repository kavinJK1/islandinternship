For Parents

We know you're the other half of this decision.

Your concerns are completely valid. Here's every answer you need, clearly.

Island Internship places European university students in structured, credit-eligible internships in Bali. We have worked with students from Maastricht, UvA, Erasmus, Tilburg, Vrije Universiteit, and Hogeschool Rotterdam. We understand what parents and university coordinators need to see before saying yes — and we've built the program around making that easy.

Your concerns, answered

Six questions most parents ask first.

Safety

Is Bali safe?

Bali is a well-established international destination with a large expat, digital nomad, and student community. The most common practical risks — traffic, scooters, upset stomachs — are managed through pre-departure safety preparation we provide to every student before they fly. This is not a dramatic destination; it is a managed one.

Emergency contact

Who do we call if something goes wrong?

We are reachable via WhatsApp throughout the placement. Emergency guidance, local contacts, and step-by-step protocols are shared with every student before departure. You will have a contact number and know exactly what to do before your child boards the plane.

University credit

Will this count for their degree?

Yes. Every host company provides an official internship agreement, a named supervisor, and the documentation Dutch universities require to approve the placement. This is compatible with Maastricht, UvA, Erasmus, Tilburg, Vrije Universiteit, and Hogeschool Rotterdam. We recommend early confirmation with the study coordinator — and we help prepare the paperwork.

Cost

How much will this actually cost?

The program fee is €449 (Essentials) or €649 (Full Support), paid once matched and confirmed. Monthly living costs in Bali run €440–€630 — which is 40–50% less than a typical month in Amsterdam or Rotterdam. For most families, Bali is not the more expensive option.

Housing

Who arranged the housing?

We do. Every student gets a private room in a vetted shared house in a central, safe area of Bali. The address is confirmed before they book flights — there are no unknowns on arrival day.

Community

Will they be alone?

No. Students arrive into an existing community of other interns from European universities. Welcome dinners, shared housing, group chats, and weekend plans are built into the program — by the end of the first week, most students already have a group around them.

Cost comparison

Bali is usually the more affordable semester.

This is one of the most surprising answers for parents. Bali is consistently cheaper than keeping your child in a Dutch university city.

Bali — estimated monthly total
€440–630

Private room, scooter, food, SIM, activities. Typically 40–50% less than staying in the Netherlands.

Amsterdam / Rotterdam — typical monthly budget
€990–1,450

Rent, food, transport, social. The internship semester still costs money at home — just more of it.

The program fee (€449–649) is a one-time payment, made only once matched and confirmed. There is no cost to apply.

Support structure

What happens at each stage of the program.

  • 1
    Pre-departure briefing

    Every student receives a detailed pre-departure pack covering visa, housing, local transport, safety tips, emergency contacts, and what to expect in the first week.

  • 2
    Arrival support

    Airport pickup (Full Support), housing check-in guidance, and orientation into the community — so the first 48 hours don't feel like a problem to solve alone.

  • 3
    WhatsApp reachability throughout

    Our team remains reachable for the duration of the placement. Practical questions, housing issues, placement concerns — nothing goes unanswered.

  • 4
    Emergency protocol

    Clear emergency guidance is shared before departure. Students know exactly who to contact and what to do — as do you.
