For Parents

You probably have more questions than your child does.

That is completely normal. Sending your child across the world for an internship is a significant decision, and the details matter. This page exists to answer every question you are likely to have — honestly and specifically — before you give the green light.

Island Internship places European university students in structured, credit-eligible internships in Bali and Sri Lanka. We have worked with students from Maastricht, UvA, Erasmus, Tilburg, Vrije Universiteit, and Hogeschool Rotterdam. We understand what parents need to see before saying yes — and we have built the program around making that easy. The application is free. There is no commitment until a placement is confirmed and you are ready to proceed.

Your concerns, answered

The six things parents ask first.

These are the questions that come up in almost every parent conversation. Each one gets a full section further down this page — but here is the short version.

Safety

Is Bali safe?

Bali is one of the most visited islands in the world, with a well-established international community. The risks that exist — traffic, motorbikes, food hygiene — are real but manageable. We provide every student with a detailed safety briefing before departure so they arrive prepared, not surprised.

Emergency contact

Who do we call if something goes wrong?

You will have our WhatsApp number before your child boards the plane. We have clear protocols for medical situations, visa problems, and early returns — and we are reachable throughout the entire placement. You are never left waiting for an answer.

University credit

Will this count for their degree?

Yes. Every host company provides an official internship agreement, a named supervisor, mid-term and end-of-placement evaluations, and the documentation Dutch universities require to approve the placement. We help prepare the paperwork students need to submit to their study coordinator.

Cost

How much will this actually cost?

The program fee is €449 (Essentials) or €649 (Full Support), paid once a placement is confirmed. Monthly living costs in Bali run €440–€630 — roughly 40–50% less than a comparable month in Amsterdam or Rotterdam. For most families, Bali is the more affordable option, not the more expensive one.

Housing

Where will they live?

In a private room in a vetted shared house in a central, safe area. The address is confirmed before your child books their flights — there are no unknowns on arrival day. We handle the housing search so students do not have to figure it out from another continent.

Community

Will they be alone?

No. Students arrive into an existing cohort of interns from European universities. Welcome dinners, shared housing, group chats, and weekend plans are built into the program. Most students have a group around them within the first few days.

From application to first day

What happens at every stage.

There are no surprises in this process. Here is exactly what happens, in order, from the moment your child applies to the end of their first week in-destination.

  • 1
    Week 1–2
    Free application & intro call

    Your child applies — no fee, no commitment. We schedule an intro call to understand their field of study, goals, and timeline. Parents are welcome on this call.

  • 2
    Week 2–4
    Matching process

    We match your child's profile against available host companies in their track. You receive details of the proposed placement — sector, company description, supervisor name — before any commitment is made.

  • 3
    Week 4–8
    Confirmed placement & visa guidance begins

    Once your child accepts a placement, the program fee is paid and the internship agreement is signed. We begin visa guidance immediately — walking through the exact steps for the B211A (Bali) or ETA (Sri Lanka). Housing is also confirmed around this point.

  • 4
    2 weeks before departure
    Pre-departure pack, housing address, emergency contacts

    Every student receives a comprehensive pre-departure document: housing address, local emergency contacts, nearest hospital, scooter safety guidance, SIM card setup, money advice, and what to expect on day one. You receive our direct WhatsApp contact for the duration of the placement.

  • 5
    Day 0 — arrival
    Airport pickup (Full Support)

    Full Support students are collected from the airport and taken directly to their accommodation. Essentials students receive step-by-step arrival instructions. No one lands without knowing exactly what happens next.

  • 6
    Week 1 in Bali
    Orientation week & workplace introduction

    The first week includes a group orientation covering local transport, safety, banking, and cultural context. Students are introduced formally to their workplace supervisor. By the end of week one, the internship is in motion and the student is settled.

Visa

The visa process is straightforward. We guide every step.

This is often the part that worries parents the most. Here is exactly what is involved for each destination.

🇮🇩
Bali, Indonesia

B211A Social-Cultural Visa

The B211A is Indonesia's social and cultural visa — a legal entry route commonly used by international interns and students. It is applied for online before departure, costs approximately $65 USD, and is issued for 60 days with the ability to extend in-country. Island Internship provides students with a step-by-step guide and all supporting documents needed for the application.

The visa is entirely above board for internship purposes. Indonesia does not issue a separate "intern visa" — the B211A is the standard legal pathway used by all international students doing internships in Bali.

We guide students through: the online portal, document preparation, sponsor letter (which we provide), and any extension if the placement runs longer than 60 days.

🇱🇰
Sri Lanka

Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA)

Sri Lanka uses an online ETA system for most European nationals. It is straightforward — applied for online, typically approved within 24 hours, and valid for 30 days with extensions available. Costs approximately $35 USD.

Island Internship provides the same visa guidance for Sri Lanka placements as for Bali — clear instructions, required documents, and support if any questions arise during the process.

As with Bali, the visa process is completed before the student travels. No surprises at the airport.

Visa rules change. We keep our guidance current and check every student's situation individually. If your child's nationality raises specific considerations, we will identify them during the matching process — not after they have booked a flight.

Insurance

Travel insurance is required. Here is what it must cover.

We do not allow students to travel without adequate insurance. This is not optional. Below is exactly what the policy needs to include, and how we help students find one that qualifies.

Medical coverage

A minimum of €100,000 in emergency medical coverage. This covers hospitalisation, surgery, doctor visits, and medication if something goes wrong in-destination. Standard travel insurance from Dutch banks and travel insurers typically meets this threshold — check the policy documents.

Emergency evacuation

The policy must cover emergency medical evacuation — meaning if a serious incident requires treatment that cannot be handled locally, the cost of flying your child home or to a regional hospital is covered. This is distinct from standard medical coverage and must be listed explicitly.

Trip cancellation / interruption

Coverage if the placement needs to end early for a covered reason — illness, family emergency, or similar. This protects the cost of flights and program fees in unforeseen circumstances. Not every policy includes this; verify it is present.

24-hour emergency helpline

The insurer must provide a 24-hour emergency assistance line that can coordinate local medical care in Indonesia or Sri Lanka. Most international travel insurers offer this — but confirm the number works in-destination before departure.

Island Internship helps every student verify that their insurance meets these requirements before departure. We review the policy summary document and flag anything that is missing. Common Dutch options that typically qualify include Centraal Beheer Reisverzekering, FBTO Reisverzekering, and Allianz Travel — but the specific tier matters. We will confirm what your child has selected covers everything required.

If your child does not yet have a policy, we provide a checklist of what to look for and can point them toward options that consistently meet the requirements for both Bali and Sri Lanka placements.

Emergency scenarios

What happens if something goes wrong.

We would rather address this directly than let you wonder. The following covers the scenarios parents most frequently ask about — not to alarm you, but because you deserve a clear answer to each one.

  • Your child gets sick — fever, food poisoning, infection

    Bali has well-established international clinics (BIMC Hospital in Kuta, BIMC Nusa Dua, and several others) staffed with English-speaking doctors. Students are provided the nearest clinic address and phone number in their pre-departure pack. Our team is reachable on WhatsApp and will help coordinate if needed. Travel insurance covers the cost of treatment — which is why adequate insurance is required before departure.

  • Your child needs to go to a hospital

    We provide the names, addresses, and phone numbers of the nearest international-standard hospitals for every placement location. We help students communicate with their insurer and, if needed, assist with coordination. In a serious medical situation, we contact parents directly. You will not find out about something serious from a WhatsApp message hours after the fact.

  • There is a visa problem

    Visa issues are almost always bureaucratic — a document not accepted, a form submitted incorrectly, an extension not processed in time. We guide students through the resolution, including liaising with the relevant office. In the rare event that an extension cannot be processed in time, students may need to do a visa run (a brief border exit); we explain this process in advance so it is never a surprise.

  • There is a problem with the host company

    If a placement is not working — the role is misrepresented, the supervisor is absent, or the environment is unsuitable — we intervene. We have relationships with multiple host companies in each destination, and a rematch is possible if a genuine placement failure occurs. Students do not have to manage a bad placement alone.

  • Your child wants to come home early

    This is not a common outcome — the majority of students extend their stay rather than cut it short. But if your child decides to come home for any reason, we help facilitate the departure: assisting with flight changes, communicating with the host company, and ensuring the handover is clean. University credit implications depend on how much of the placement was completed, and we help manage the communication with the student's study coordinator.

  • A natural event or security situation in-destination

    Bali and Sri Lanka are not high-risk destinations for political instability. For seismic activity (Bali sits on an active volcanic island), students receive a clear briefing on local protocols before departure. We monitor FCDO and Rijksoverheid travel advisories and contact students proactively if anything changes. In a situation requiring evacuation, travel insurance emergency evacuation coverage applies.

We are reachable throughout your child's placement.

Sri Lanka — what's the same

Same support, different island

Vetted host companies, official internship agreements, pre-departure briefing, in-destination WhatsApp support, housing arranged before arrival, and the same university documentation structure. The ETA visa process is simpler than Bali's B211A — straightforward online application, typically approved within a day.

Sri Lanka — placement tracks

Similar tracks, growing partner network

Sri Lanka placements currently span marketing, business, hospitality, and sustainability-focused organisations. The intern community is smaller than Bali's but growing. For students interested in a slightly different cultural context — South Asian rather than Southeast Asian — Sri Lanka is a strong alternative.

Sri Lanka — cost

Comparable to Bali, sometimes lower

Monthly living costs in Sri Lanka are broadly comparable to Bali, sometimes slightly lower depending on the city. The same program fee structure applies. University credit documentation follows the same format used for Bali placements.

Support structure

The support that exists throughout the entire placement.

This is not a program where a student receives a placement, a packet of documents, and is then left alone for three months. Here is what the ongoing support actually looks like.

  • 1
    Pre-departure briefing document

    Every student receives a detailed pre-departure pack covering visa steps, housing address, local transport, safety tips, nearest hospital, emergency contacts, banking, SIM card setup, and what to expect in the first week. It is comprehensive and specific — not a generic checklist.

  • 2
    Airport pickup & housing check-in (Full Support)

    Full Support students are collected from the airport and taken to their accommodation. Essentials students receive detailed arrival instructions so that even without pickup, the first 48 hours are managed.

  • 3
    Orientation week

    The first week includes a structured group orientation — local transport, cultural context, safety reminders, and a group dinner with other interns. Students are not dropped into an unfamiliar place and left to work it out.

  • 4
    WhatsApp reachability throughout the placement

    Our team remains reachable for the full duration of every placement. Housing issues, workplace questions, visa extension support, medical coordination — nothing goes unanswered. Response time is same-day for all non-emergency queries, and immediate for urgent situations.

  • 5
    End-of-placement documentation support

    We help students collect the evaluations, signed agreements, and supervisor sign-offs their university needs to approve the credit. This is handled in the final weeks of the placement so there is no scramble at the end.
