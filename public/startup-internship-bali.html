Startup & Operations Internship in Bali.
Work inside a real company where your output actually moves things.

Most operations and business students spend their internship semester shadowing. Startup environments in Bali don't work like that — small teams with real pressure mean you take on tasks from day one. If you want an internship where you leave with specific, nameable contributions rather than a long list of things you observed, this track is the right fit.

  • Matched to early-stage and growth-stage startups where interns work close to founders and operators.
  • Official internship agreement, named supervisor, and university documentation — credit-eligible from day one.

What you'll do

Operational and strategic work inside fast-moving teams.

Startup and operations interns in Bali contribute directly to how the business runs — not what it plans to run one day.

Startup operations

Keep the business running smoothly

Own operational tasks — scheduling, supplier coordination, process management, and the day-to-day execution that keeps a growing company on track.

Process improvement

Spot and fix operational inefficiencies

Map existing workflows, identify friction points, and propose or implement improvements that save the team time and energy.

Project coordination

Lead projects across functions

Coordinate cross-functional projects — keeping stakeholders aligned, tracking timelines, and ensuring deliverables land on time.

Growth support

Founder-facing support on strategic tasks

Work directly with founders and senior operators on high-priority projects — the kind of exposure that typically takes years to reach in larger organisations.

What you leave with

Career proof that follows you after the internship ends.

CV proof

Named projects and operational outcomes

Specific projects you owned, processes you improved, and outcomes you can measure — the kind of CV detail that stands out to anyone who has hired before.

Business exposure

Inside the operational reality of a startup

Commercial awareness built by being inside the decisions — not reading about them in a case study.

International network

Contacts in the startup and operator community

Founder relationships, co-intern connections, and a professional network that spans industries and nationalities.

Career direction

Know whether startups are your environment

Leave knowing if you want the pace and ownership of early-stage companies — or if you prefer a more structured corporate path.
