What a week actually looks like

A typical week in a Bali marketing internship.

Mornings are usually quiet. Most teams start around 9–10am. You work in English with an international team, contribute to live marketing output, and wrap the day with a full evening ahead.

Monday – Tuesday

Campaigns and content in progress

Most marketing interns start the week on active campaigns — scheduled content, email sequences, or performance reports from the previous week. Teams move fast and expect real contribution early.

Wednesday

Team meetings and feedback

Mid-week check-ins, results reviews, and planning sessions. This is often when you get visibility into how the team makes decisions — useful for any role you take after graduation.

Thursday – Friday

Output and delivery

Finishing deliverables, publishing content, or briefing the next week's work. Most interns note that Fridays feel productive rather than quiet — the pace stays consistent through the week.

Outside work hours

Canggu, surf, and community

Most interns live and work around Canggu. Evenings and weekends involve cafes, surf sessions, community dinners, gym sessions, and weekend trips across Bali — a meaningful backdrop to the placement.
