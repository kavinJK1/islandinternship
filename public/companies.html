What Island Internship handles

We take care of the recruitment and arrival side.

Your team defines the role. We surface the right candidates, manage the documentation, and ensure students are prepared before they start. You get meaningful contributions without the overhead.

Sourcing

Profile matching and shortlisting

We review applications and shortlist students who fit the role, the team culture, and the location — so your team only evaluates strong matches.

Documentation

University paperwork and agreements

We coordinate the university learning agreement, internship approval documentation, and any forms your company needs for a compliant placement.

Arrival

Visa, housing, and orientation

Students arrive prepared — visa arranged, housing confirmed, and an orientation week in place before they join your team on day one.

Ongoing

Support throughout the placement

We stay in contact during the placement to address any questions and help resolve logistics, so your team can focus on the work itself.

How the partner process works.

Becoming a host company is straightforward. Once we understand your team and the kind of role you want to fill, we begin sourcing and keep you involved at every key decision point.

1

Tell us about the role

Share the kind of work you need support with, your team size, preferred start timing, and any specific study background that would be a good fit.

2

We shortlist and introduce

We review the current intake, identify suitable profiles, and make warm introductions. You meet candidates — not a long pipeline of cold applications.

3

Placement confirmed and student arrives

Once you confirm a match, we handle the agreements, arrival logistics, and orientation. Your new intern is ready to contribute from the first week.

Who you'll be working with

Motivated students from leading Dutch universities.

Students join Island Internship because they actively chose to do their mandatory university internship abroad. They arrive having already managed a visa application, relocation, and preparation — a different baseline than a local placement.

Background

Business, marketing, and operations

The majority of students come from business administration, international management, marketing, hospitality, and related programmes at Dutch universities and universities of applied sciences.

Language

English-proficient and internationally oriented

Students are selected for English-language work readiness. Many have prior international experience through exchange programmes or travel.

Duration

3 to 6 month placements

Standard durations are 3, 4, 5, or 6 months — aligned with Dutch university internship requirements. We can discuss timing to match your team's project cycle or seasonal needs.

Universities

Maastricht, Erasmus, Breda, and more

Current students come from Maastricht University, Erasmus University Rotterdam, Breda University of Applied Sciences, and other Dutch institutions with mandatory internship programmes.

What host companies gain from the partnership.

International interns bring fresh perspective, academic rigour, and genuine commitment. Companies that host through Island Internship often find the arrangement works well precisely because the students chose to be there.

  • A motivated candidate who chose an international internship and has prepared for the move.
  • University documentation and agreements handled — you don't manage the paperwork.
  • Flexible timing across intakes throughout the year — not tied to a fixed academic calendar.
  • An ongoing relationship — companies that work well together often partner across multiple intakes.
